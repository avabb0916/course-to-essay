# Extracting the raw material

An essay is built from a point of view, not a pile of facts. Before writing,
pull these six things out of the course into a scratch note. If you can't find
#1 and #2, re-read — a course always has them, even if implicit.

## The six things

### 1. Core thesis
The single belief the whole course argues for. One sentence. Everything else
serves this. (Example: "In the AI era, your private data and tacit know-how are
the only real moat.")

### 2. The "wrong" mainstream view it corrects
Almost every course opens by pushing against a comfortable, widely-held
assumption. This is the essay's tension — the "P" in APAG. Look for phrases like
"很多人以为…", "一个普遍的主流观点", "你是不是觉得…", "most people think…".
(Example: "people believe they already understand data" → 习惯性傲慢.)

### 3. Frameworks / models
Named structures: step lists, acronym models, "N不变M巨变", matrices, pyramids,
flywheels. These become the essay's section architecture. Capture the name AND
what each part means in plain words. (Example: ADAPTED 6+1; 3不变3巨变; DIKW.)

### 4. Concrete cases / stories
Real examples, before/after arcs, named people, hard numbers. These are proof
and texture — they keep an idea-heavy essay from feeling abstract. Capture
enough detail to retell briefly. (Example: 讲故事的爸爸 building a story
data-pack; 500万→5万 cost collapse; the 美业 company's 5-stage data climb.)

### 5. Quotable lines
The punchlines the instructor emphasized (often visually highlighted). These
become section payoffs and your closer. Capture verbatim, then you can sharpen.
(Example: "对于一个坚定的长期主义者来说，所有短期的红利和机会都是假的，AI 数据
才是你最值得攒的真大牌。")

### 6. Action steps
What the course tells people to actually DO. These power the "Gamify" section.
Often the framework doubles as the steps.

## Checklist before writing

- [ ] I can state the thesis in one sentence.
- [ ] I have the specific wrong-belief the essay will dismantle.
- [ ] I have 1–3 frameworks with each part in plain language.
- [ ] I have at least 2 concrete cases with enough detail to retell.
- [ ] I have 3–5 quotable lines, including a candidate closer.
- [ ] I know the do-able steps for the action section.

## What to drop

Course scaffolding that doesn't belong in an essay: interaction prompts
(【互动】, "大家猜猜"), warm-up questions, "扫码加入", homework sections, page
numbers, repeated calls to the live audience. Keep the ideas, lose the
classroom.

## Multiple PDFs on the same topic

When the user uploads several PDFs covering the same subject (e.g. a main
lecture + a follow-up + a different speaker's take), read all of them but
produce ONE unified extraction note, not one per PDF. Merge overlapping
frameworks, collect the best cases from all sources, and note where the
sources agree vs. where they emphasize different things. The authorial layer
benefits enormously from multiple inputs — you have more angles to choose from
and more material to reorganize under your own frame.
